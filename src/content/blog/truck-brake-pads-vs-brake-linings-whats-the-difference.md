Brake pads and brake linings are both friction materials used in vehicle braking systems, but they are not the same thing.

For commercial vehicle buyers, confusing these two terms can lead to quotation mistakes, fitment mismatch, and incorrect ordering.

Understanding the difference is important when sourcing truck braking components.

---

## 1. Brake Pads Are Used in Disc Brake Systems

Brake pads are friction components used in **disc brake** systems.

In a disc brake setup:

- the brake disc rotates with the wheel  
- the caliper presses brake pads against the disc  
- friction slows the vehicle  

Pads are usually mounted inside a caliper assembly and are easier to inspect visually compared with enclosed drum-brake friction parts.

---

## 2. Brake Linings Are Used in Drum Brake Systems

Brake linings are friction materials attached to **brake shoes** in drum brake systems.

In a drum brake setup:

- the brake shoe expands outward  
- the lining contacts the inside of the brake drum  
- friction creates braking force  

In heavy-duty trucks, brake linings remain especially relevant because drum brakes are still common in many commercial applications.

## 4. Materials and Wear Characteristics

Both brake pads and brake linings rely on friction-material formulation, but the application environment is different.

Factors that matter include:

- heat resistance  
- wear rate  
- bonding quality  
- friction stability  

Heavy commercial-vehicle use places strong demands on braking materials because of:

- repeated load cycles  
- high operating weight  
- long-distance usage  

That means buyers should evaluate quality consistency, not only unit price.

---

## 5. Maintenance and Replacement Differences

Brake pad replacement is often simpler because disc-brake components are more accessible.

Brake lining replacement in drum systems may involve:

- shoe inspection  
- drum-condition review  
- more disassembly time  

For fleet operators and distributors, replacement cycle and labor convenience can influence product preference and demand patterns.

## 11. FAQ: Brake Pads vs Brake Linings

### Are brake linings and brake shoes the same thing?

No. The lining is the friction material, while the shoe is the structural carrier used in drum-brake systems.

### Are brake pads used in drum brakes?

No. Brake pads are associated with disc-brake systems.

### Why do some buyers confuse the two terms?

Because both are friction products in the braking category, and some markets use broad or inconsistent terminology.

### What should buyers confirm first when requesting a quotation?

The brake-system type, exact application, and fitment details.
